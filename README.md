# ALGORAD.IO
Filter the world's best indie radio stations by mood, genre, or weather. Curated by a human, built by an algorithm.

Today’s musical algorithms are designed to keep us addicted to subscriptions and to push major label artist drops, trapping us in soul-numbing content loops while the indie artists and communities who actually create music culture are left behind.

ALGORAD.IO is an attempt to use advanced technology for something better.

The internet is built on algorithms, but they aren't all dystopian by design. Some algorithms can be used for utopian purposes – like the ones that host independent radio streams, or the co-coding tools that allow a non-coder like me to build a platform to celebrate my favourite music communities.

Designed to live amongst your browser tabs, ALGORAD.IO lets you filter my favourite internet radio stations by mood, genre, or weather. Choose whatever fits your current vibe.
In the age of Algotopia, this is my way of using technology to highlight human curation and return to the foundations of the internet: community and creativity.

Who’s The Selector?

My name’s Tim and I’m an algoholic.

I’ve also spent a lifetime being obsessed with the radio, as both a listener and host.

My broadcasting journey began producing student radio at London Guildhall University in the late 90s. This foundation led to a career scouting new talent for John Kennedy’s X-Posure show on Radio X, curating new music playlists for Dazed Digital, leading an editorial team at Boiler Room, and being a repeat guest on BBC Radio 1’s Review Show. I was also one of the original DJs at NTS Radio, hosting the Synth Hero Show from the station's launch in 2011 through to 2019.

ALGORAD.IO is my latest audio adventure – a human curated sidequest co-coded with AI to discover and listen to my favourite indie stations in a new way. I’ve really enjoyed making it, so I thought I’d share it with other like-minded music fans. 

There’s links to all of the stations in the index, so click through and support them.

– Tim Noakes
